Software teams have more tools than ever. AI writes code. Design systems scale. Product managers have roadmaps, backlogs, specs.

And yet — ask anyone on the team to describe the whole product, how it all fits together, what was built and why — and you'll get a different answer from every person you ask.

The tools got better. The shared understanding didn't.

Skelly is a bet that the missing piece isn't another tool for one role. It's a shared model of the product itself — one that every person and every AI on the team can draw from.
